User Re-boarding Access Automation

Product Owner / SharePoint Solutions Architect

Power Automate SharePoint Online Microsoft Graph API Process Automation User Experience

Context

When employees re-joined the company, they encountered access issues with SharePoint sites and files that had been previously shared with them during their earlier tenure. Their old account traces remained in the system, creating permission conflicts.

Problem

Re-joining employees had to raise helpdesk tickets and wait days for resolution. The manual two-step process required: (1) IT support to manually identify and remove old account traces from sites, and (2) contacting site owners to re-share access. This time-consuming, multi-handoff workflow delayed productivity and created frustration for re-joiners, helpdesk teams, and site owners alike.

Goal

Transform the days-long manual process into a minutes-long automated workflow. Eliminate helpdesk dependency for step 1 (cleanup), streamline step 2 (re-sharing requests), reduce support burden, and ensure re-joining employees have rapid access to their required resources.

Users & Stakeholders

  • Re-joining employees
  • IT support team
  • HR operations
  • Site owners & administrators
  • Business unit managers

Product Owner Approach

Problem Discovery & Quantification

Analyzed helpdesk ticket data to quantify wait times (avg. 3-5 days). Interviewed re-joiners, IT support, and site owners to map the manual two-step workflow and identify bottlenecks at each handoff.

Stakeholder Alignment

Facilitated workshops with HR, IT, and business units to define requirements, success criteria, and prioritize use cases.

Backlog Creation

Built and prioritized user stories focusing on highest-impact scenarios: site access, file sharing, and permission cleanup.

Incremental Delivery

MVP 1 was released with automation to remove old traces of user account. MVP 2 is planned which will automate site and file sharing access.

Success Metrics Definition

Established KPIs: ticket reduction, time-to-access, error rates, and user satisfaction scores.

Continuous Improvement

Monitored post-launch metrics, conducted retrospectives, and iterated based on support team and user feedback.

Solution

Designed and delivered a Power Automate workflow that:

Identifies Old Account Traces

Scans SharePoint sites and libraries to locate permissions associated with the employee's previous account.

Automates Permission Cleanup (Step 1)

Replaces manual helpdesk work with automated removal of old account traces across sites, libraries, and files—reducing days of waiting to minutes of processing.

Notifies Stakeholders

Sends confirmation to affected user and IT support team.

Compliance Logging

Maintains logs of all actions that automation took for governance and troubleshooting.

Outcomes

Days to Minutes

Reduced resolution time from 3-5 days of waiting for helpdesk to just minutes of automated processing—transforming the re-joiner experience.

85% Ticket Reduction

Eliminated the majority of re-boarding access tickets, freeing IT support for higher-value work and reducing operational costs.

Same-Day Access

Re-joining employees no longer wait days for helpdesk resolution—they have seamless access on their first day, improving onboarding experience.

Zero Manual Errors

Automated process eliminated human error in permission cleanup and re-sharing, ensuring consistency.

Product Owner Activities

  • User Research: Shadowed helpdesk operations during manual cleanup, observed the multi-day wait cycles, and documented handoff delays between IT and site owners
  • Data Analysis: Quantified the problem using ticket metrics: average 3-5 day resolution time, peak volumes during re-hiring seasons, and cost per manual intervention
  • Requirements Gathering: Documented edge cases like partial access, guest users, and external sharing through collaborative workshops with IT and site owners
  • Prioritization: Used value vs. effort matrix to sequence features: Step 1 (automated cleanup) delivered immediate value, Step 2 (streamlined re-sharing) added convenience, notifications provided transparency
  • Acceptance Criteria: Defined clear, testable criteria for each user story with IT and security teams
  • Sprint Planning: Collaborated with development team to break work into 2-week sprints
  • Demo & Feedback: Conducted bi-weekly demos with stakeholders to validate progress and pivot as needed
  • Metrics Tracking: Built dashboard to monitor ticket volume, resolution time, and user satisfaction

Key Artifacts

  • Product Backlog: Prioritized user stories with acceptance criteria
  • User Journey Maps: Before/after state for re-joining employees
  • Process Flow Diagrams: Manual vs automated workflow comparison
  • Success Metrics Dashboard: Real-time tracking of KPIs
  • Stakeholder Presentations: Business value and ROI demonstrations

Evolution Journey

Before: Manual two-step process taking 3-5 days with multiple helpdesk handoffs

Phase 1: Process observation and data-driven problem quantification

Phase 2: MVP 1 - Automated Step 1 (remove old traces of user account) reducing wait time to minutes

Phase 3: MVP 2 - Planned - Enhanced Step 2 with intelligent re-sharing and site owner notifications

Phase 4: Full integration with HR onboarding for proactive access provisioning

Key Takeaways

This project exemplifies product ownership at its core: using data to quantify a critical pain point (3-5 day wait times), building multi-stakeholder consensus across IT, site owners, and HR, and delivering incremental value through phased automation. By transforming a manual, multi-day process into a minutes-long automated workflow, we not only reduced operational costs but also dramatically improved the re-joiner experience during a critical moment. The data-driven, iterative approach allowed us to prove value quickly (Step 1 automation), then expand based on measured impact rather than assumptions (Step 2 enhancement), ultimately creating a business case for broader HR integration.
