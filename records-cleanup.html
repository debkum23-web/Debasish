Records Cleanup Automation

Product Owner perspective on eliminating stale inventory noise

Product Ownership Process Automation Data Quality Stakeholder Management Power Automate

Context

Inventory reports were cluttered with records that were inactive or untouched for over a year. Stakeholders struggled to analyze data or make decisions because obsolete items distorted metrics and priorities.

Problem

Stale records remained visible to business and compliance teams, creating noise, inflating counts, and slowing decisions. Manual cleanup was ad hoc, inconsistent, and rarely prioritized.

Goal

Automate identification of stale records, engage record owners for confirmation, and decommission obsolete items so the inventory reflects only actionable, current records.

Stakeholders

  • Record owners
  • Business unit leads
  • Compliance / governance partners
  • Data & reporting teams
  • IT / platform admins

Product Owner Approach

Discovery & Quantification

Analyzed inventory freshness and found high volume of 12+ month inactive records; mapped impact on reporting accuracy and decision latency.

Stakeholder Alignment

Ran workshops with record owners, BU leads, and compliance to agree on freshness thresholds, approval flow, and decommission rules.

Backlog & Prioritization

Sequenced work: detection MVP, owner notification loop, decommission automation, dashboards for auditability.

Incremental Delivery

Shipped MVP to flag stale records; iterated to add owner approvals and automated decommission once benefits were proven.

Metrics & ROI

Tracked noise reduction (% stale removed), response time from owners, and improved report accuracy as success measures.

Change Management

Communicated policy, provided opt-out safeguards, and ensured audit trails satisfied governance requirements.

Solution

Built an automated flow to continuously keep the inventory clean and decision-ready:

Detect Stale Records

Flags records untouched for 12+ months using last-updated metadata and configurable thresholds.

Engage Record Owners

Sends targeted notifications requesting confirmation to keep or decommission flagged records.

Decommission on Approval

Automatically decommissions records after owner approval (or timeout policy), removing them from active inventory views.

Audit & Compliance

Logs every decision and action for governance, enabling traceability and reporting.

Noise-Free Reporting

Feeds cleansed inventory to dashboards so stakeholders see only current, actionable records.

Outcomes

Noise Reduction

Removed a large backlog of stale records, improving data quality and signal-to-noise for stakeholders.

Faster Decisions

Stakeholders could act on cleaner inventory data without wading through obsolete items.

Owner Accountability

Owners became engaged stewards through the approval loop, increasing trust in the inventory.

Key Product Owner Responsibilities

  • Frame the problem with data (staleness rates, reporting impact).
  • Align policy with compliance and business owners.
  • Prioritize MVPs to prove value before expanding scope.
  • Define success metrics and monitor adoption and ROI.
  • Ensure change management, communication, and safeguards.

Phase 2: MVP detection with owner notifications.

Phase 4: Reporting refresh with cleansed inventory.
